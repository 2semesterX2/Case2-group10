Antique Museum

Welcome to the Antikmuseet

The Antique museum is a university museum. It was established as a study collection in 1949 by the first professor of classical archeology at Aarhus University, PJ Riis. The museum's collection consists of more than 500 plaster casts and 4000 original objects from the entire Mediterranean, covering a period from the early Bronze Age to Roman Empire.

About us

Welcome to the Antikmuseet

The Antique museum is a university museum. It was established as a study collection in 1949 by the first professor of classical archeology at Aarhus University, PJ Riis. The museum's collection consists of more than 500 plaster casts and 4000 original objects from the entire Mediterranean, covering a period from the early Bronze Age to Roman Empire.

Schools and gymnasiums are also more than welcome but have to set up the visit in advance. The museum has an elevator for the disabled. It is free to visit the museum for all the visitors. Guided tours have to be set up in advance and cost 500dkr.
